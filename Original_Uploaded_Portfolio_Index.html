01 — Measurable Policy Problem

UAE Context: Mobility Productivity, Safety, Emissions, and Value of Time.

The portfolio focuses on variables that can support economic evaluation: delay, speed, travel-time reliability, crashes, emissions, CAPEX/OPEX, and value of time. UAE context is used as a policy and data environment, not as cultural decoration.

Transport Outcomes

The design measures delay, speed loss, travel-time reliability, crashes, and emissions before translating them into economic value.

Public Cost and Benefit

Investment is evaluated through CAPEX/OPEX, value of time, safety, emissions, NPV, BCR, and sensitivity analysis.

Data Constraints

The design recognizes that timing, geography, and control data may be incomplete, so it defines fallback methods before making causal claims.

04 — Identification & Appraisal & Policy Evaluation

Methods: identification first, appraisal second, forecasting third.

The research design starts with causal identification, not AI. DiD/event study is attempted only if treatment timing, geography, outcomes, and controls are defensible. If not, the design downgrades to DLD hedonic fallback or scenario appraisal.

Treatment Registry

Intervention timing, corridor/intersection geography, exposure buffers, and pre/post outcome windows.

Causal Identification

DiD/event study where parallel-trend and control requirements are credible; otherwise no causal claim.

Welfare Appraisal

CBA using CAPEX/OPEX, value of time, safety, emissions, NPV/BCR, and sensitivity analysis.

Forecasting & MCDA Evidence

XGBoost and AROMAN remain supporting evidence from prior work, not substitutes for policy identification.

Data-governance protocol: the research design applies data minimization, purpose limitation, source logging, anonymization or aggregation where feasible, retention limits, access control, and documented treatment of cross-border processing risks. AI-assisted components will be accompanied by model-card style documentation, assumption logs, sensitivity analysis, and human review. Ethical claims will be restricted to what the available data, institutional approvals, and UAE legal framework can support.
